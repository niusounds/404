# Horror Post Reviewer

This skill provides a structured framework for evaluating the "horror level" of blog posts, specifically tailored for the Japanese horror aesthetic (J-Horror, Hitokowa, Mystery).

## Review Workflow

1.  **Analyze Tone & Genre**: Determine if the post is Supernatural, Mystery, or Hitokowa (human horror).
2.  **Evaluate Core Metrics**: Assess the draft using the established 4-point horror scale. See [references/criteria.md](references/criteria.md) for details.
3.  **Identify "Resonating" Elements**: Point out specific phrases or descriptions that effectively trigger fear.
4.  **Propose Psychological Escalation**: Suggest ways to increase tension through sensory details or "unreliable narrator" techniques.

## Specific Instructions

- **Focus on "Ma" (Negative Space)**: In Japanese horror, what is *not* shown is often scarier than what is. Suggest removing explicit descriptions to leave more to the imagination.
- **Sensory Overload**: If a draft is too visual, suggest adding auditory or olfactory triggers (e.g., the smell of wet soil, the sound of a dragging foot).
- **Subtle Reality Distortion**: Look for opportunities to introduce small, logical inconsistencies that suggest reality is breaking down.
