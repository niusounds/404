# Horror Evaluation Criteria

Detailed breakdown of the metrics used to evaluate horror stories for the Kowai Site.

## 1. Fear Score (恐怖の強さ)
Measures the immediate visceral reaction.
- **High Score**: Physical symptoms (shivers, increased heart rate).
- **Techniques**: Biological threats, immediate proximity of the unknown, breakdown of safe spaces (home, bed).

## 2. Novelty (新鮮さ)
Measures how much the story avoids common tropes.
- **High Score**: New technology-based horror, unique social situations, or subverting traditional J-Horror tropes (like well-ghosts or long-haired women).
- **Check**: Has this been done in the last 10 posts? (Cross-reference AGENTS.md).

## 3. Immersion (没入感)
Measures the realism and "presence" of the story.
- **High Score**: First-person perspective with mundane details that make the horror feel plausible.
- **Techniques**: Using specific brand names, real-world locations (or slightly distorted ones), and realistic dialogue.

## 4. Aftertaste (残響力)
Measures the lingering fear after the story ends.
- **High Score**: The reader looks over their shoulder or avoids certain actions (like looking in a mirror) after reading.
- **Techniques**: Open endings, "it's not over," or "this could happen to you."

---

## Sub-Genre Guidelines

### Hitokowa (Human Horror)
- Focus on obsessive behavior, moral decay, or hidden malice.
- The "scary" part is often the realization of a person's true nature.

### Mystery Horror
- Focus on an unexplainable event or a strange item.
- Fear comes from the *process* of investigation and the horrifying implications of the truth.

### Supernatural Horror
- Focus on the breaking of physical laws.
- Fear comes from the powerlessness against an entity that doesn't follow human logic.
